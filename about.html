Giant pandas are identified by their distinctive black and
white coloring. Their ears, muzzle, eyes, shoulders and legs are black
while the rest of their body is white. Their thick hair keeps them warm in the cool, wet mountain zones.
When on all fours, giant pandas average between 60-100 cm (2-3 ft.) tall at the shoulder and between 1-2 m (4-6 ft.) long.
They can weigh between 100-115 kg (220-250 lb.), with males being larger than females.
